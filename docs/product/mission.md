# Shipwrecked Pools App Product Mission

## What This App Is
The Shipwrecked Pools app is a premium customer app and internal operating system for Shipwrecked Pools. It is designed to help homeowners understand what is happening with their specific pool and to help the Shipwrecked team run service operations with consistent quality, speed, and accountability.

It is not just a customer portal. It is a shared system of record for customer communication, route progress, service reports, service photos, chemistry readings, requests, quotes, approvals, repairs, billing, and operational history.

## Why Shipwrecked Pools Is Building It
Shipwrecked Pools has grown by being reliable, communicative, honest, and professional. The app exists to strengthen those exact advantages as the company scales.

Shipwrecked is building this system to:
- Deliver a cleaner and more confidence-building customer experience.
- Reduce customer uncertainty after every visit.
- Standardize technician and admin workflows.
- Improve training and delegation as the team grows.
- Build a proprietary operating system that can eventually replace Skimmer.

## Core Customer Value: Confidence and Visibility
The product is fundamentally about customer confidence, not just data display.

Customers should move from uncertainty to clarity:
- What was done at the property.
- What was observed.
- What chemistry was measured and adjusted.
- What decisions are needed next.
- What service, repair, or product actions are recommended.

The app should help a homeowner feel that their pool is being cared for, understood, documented, and protected.

## V1 Anchor: Custom Top-Down Pool Outline
The custom top-down pool outline is a central V1 feature.

It is the visual anchor that connects customers to their actual pool, not a generic template. This feature should make service points, issues, photos, and recommendations easier to understand in context, increasing trust and decision speed.

## Product Areas This System Must Support
The mission includes a complete service-to-decision workflow across:
- Customer communication and human-answered questions.
- Route progress (stops-before-you and ETA, not exact live GPS).
- Service reports and customer-friendly notes.
- Service photos and history.
- Chemistry readings and chemical additions.
- Quote requests, quote approvals, typed signatures, audit logging, and payment flow.
- Repair workflows and repair reports.
- Billing and payment visibility for approved roles.
- Customer records, property context, access notes, and pet notes.
- Product and deal opportunities tied to real pool/equipment context.

## Operational Systemization and Skimmer Replacement
The app should become Shipwrecked’s operating system over time.

It should systematize recurring work, reduce tribal knowledge, and increase consistency in how routes, visits, reports, recommendations, and follow-ups are executed. The long-term intent is to replace Skimmer for routing, reports, photos, chemistry, billing, and customer communication once feature and reliability parity is reached.

## Privacy, Permissions, and Role Boundaries
Role boundaries are a core product rule, not a secondary detail.

- Customers: Can see only their own household/property data.
- Technicians: Can see assigned operational details (for example gate access notes, pet notes, phone numbers, reminders) but must not see billing, payment data, route profitability, customer profitability, or sensitive financial business data.
- Admin/Owner roles: Can access business and financial workflows according to role.

Sensitive actions should be auditable.

## Data and History as a Strategic Asset
The system should accumulate high-quality historical data that helps Shipwrecked operate better and make better decisions:
- Pool and equipment profiles.
- Visit and chemistry history.
- Repairs, approvals, and outcomes.
- Route behavior and service consistency.
- Customer communication and decision patterns.
- Product/deal needs and acceptance trends.

This historical depth supports better customer outcomes, stronger operations, and long-term company value.

## Future SaaS Optionality
The architecture should preserve optionality for future multi-company use.

Even while optimizing for Shipwrecked first, core data models and service boundaries should avoid hardcoded assumptions that prevent future SaaS packaging.

## What This App Should Not Become
This app should not become:
- A generic, template-style pool portal with weak operational depth.
- An AI-first support replacement in V1; customer questions must be answered by humans.
- A technician interface that exposes business financial data.
- A cluttered, gimmicky experience that undermines trust.
- A feature set disconnected from real service execution and customer decisions.

## Product Promise
Shipwrecked Pools helps homeowners feel confident that their pool is being cared for, understood, documented, and protected while giving the Shipwrecked team a systemized, auditable operating platform that scales.
